Chartholdr.io

Chartholdr is a quick and easy placeholder image service that delivers lorem ipsum charts and graphs. Similar to placehold.it, Chartholdr allows you to use short URLs to input chart PNGs into your website.

<img style="float:right;" src="http://chartholdr.io/pie/200">

I first had the idea for Chartholdr when presenting an early web design to a client. The days leading up to the presentation were spent building the layout, which consisted of blank placehold.it images as well as adorable placekitten images. I stared at the site so long that I forgot there were cats and never considered it could give the client the wrong image of his future site. The meeting was pretty awkward and I desperately wished there was an image placeholder solution that could deliver professional content that still looked nice.

So, I created Chartholdr.

Chartholdr is built with Ruby on Rails and is hosted on Elastic Beanstalk. The project is open source and is continually supported by me and the rest of the BrainStorm Trust. If you're curious about the nitty gritty details, check out my technical blog post about Chartholdr here.

json_resume

When I started my first technical internship May of 2014, I decided to build a personal website to gain experience with modern web technologies. I was searching for a way to include my resume and came across the json_resume project. I loved the idea of being able to include the resume as html, rather than linking to a pdf for download.

After the first few weeks of working with the PhoneGap platform at my internship, I was living and breathing responsive design. I quickly decided to expand json_resume to support mobile screen sizes. I made json_resume fully-responsive, minified sources, and added some personal styling of my own.

To see a before and after comparison as well as an introduction to grunticon, check out my technical blog post about json_resume here.
